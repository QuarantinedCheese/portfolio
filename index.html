About me:

Hello! I am Ethan Pothier, currently a Humber Polytechnic student, in my first year of the Computer Programming and Analysis program. I graduated from Sydenham Highschool, after focusing on computer tech classes and advanced math.

I'd like to take my interest in computer software into my career in the future, preferably backend. At least when it comes to websites, I do not like doing frontend. I've been apart of a few coding competetions through the years but I've never placed anywhere too impressive.

Should I change my mind or this doesn't work out, I'd love to try and make writing a career, too! I've been practicing in any freetime I can gather, and I enjoy it a lot. It's fun to take random stories that pop into my head and put them into words. So far I haven't finished anything too long, only about 4-5k words, but I'm still practicing!

Other than what I've already mentioned, i'm interested biking and playing video games! The 'touch grass' meme is kind of funny to me, especially when I've just gotten back from a ride and I hop on a game to wind down, only to be told to 'touch grass' simply because I've played the game over a few years. The main games I've been playing recently are: Brick Rigs, Rimworld, and Deep Rock Galactic.

Brick rigs allows me to build almost whatever I want, but it's an indie game focused on the creation of vehicles! Originally it had a lego-like style, with lego-like bricks, but over time it's evolved to be separate. As long as you're willing to put in the time and effort in teaching yourself a new technique, you can build anything you haven't before! Rimworld, I just enjoy a lot. It's a 2D city builder essentially, where you found a colony with an ultimate goal in mind. You don't have to complete that goal though; if you'd like you can just build the colony of your dreams! That's mostly what I do in it. Deep Rock Galactic is a game I've been playing as a main way to play with friends, it's just a lot of fun! To quickly summarise, you are a dwarf working in a mining company, and you dive deep underground to complete objectives, but beware of giant bugs!

Anyway, I feel like this ended up becoming more about the games than it does me, but I felt it would be helpful to use my current favourite games as a way to explain myself a little more. Maybe once I've got more accomplishments to mention, this will become less about games, and more about me!
